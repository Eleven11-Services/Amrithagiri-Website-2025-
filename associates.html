Kerala University

The University of Kerala is a premier institution of higher education and is often called the mother university of the state. It stands out among Indian universities for its academic excellence, innovative research, and societal commitment. Offering a wide range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and research programs across disciplines like arts, science, commerce, law, and engineering, the university has made significant contributions to Indian culture and heritage. Renowned for its cutting-edge research in space science, biotechnology, and environmental studies, the university ranks 38 overall and 21 among universities in the NIRF (National Institutional Ranking Framework). Its distinguished alumni include M. S. Swaminathan, the father of the Green Revolution, former president K. R. Narayanan, and the eminent poet Sri O. N. V. Kurup. Amrithagiri Himalaya is honored to collaborate with the Department of Political Science at the University of Kerala to study and document the Ganga River, a vital step in preserving the cultural history of India and promoting sustainable development. This partnership aligns with Amrithagiri Himalaya’s mission to support volunteer organizations, foster education NGO initiatives, and empower communities through the cultural heritage of India.

Hemvati Nandan Bahuguna Garhwal University

Hemvati Nandan Bahuguna Garhwal University (HNBGU) is a prestigious central university located in Srinagar, Uttarakhand, India. Established in 1973 and named after the renowned freedom fighter Hemvati Nandan Bahuguna, the university is set amidst the serene Garhwal Himalayas, offering a unique environment for higher education and research. HNBGU provides a wide array of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs in disciplines like arts, science, commerce, management, and technology. Known for its commitment to academic excellence, innovative research, and the preservation of Himalayan culture, the university plays a crucial role in promoting sustainable development and empowering local communities. As a leading educational hub in Uttarakhand, HNBGU attracts students from across the country, fostering holistic learning and cultural enrichment.

Sri Ganga Gai Seva Samiti

Sri Ganga Gai Seva Samiti is dedicated to protecting the Ganga River through initiatives focused on cleanliness, conservation, and sustainable practices. By preserving the river’s spiritual and environmental significance, the Samiti plays a crucial role in maintaining its purity and vitality. At the Shatrughana Temple Ghat in Varanasi, a sacred site named after Lord Rama’s brother, the Samiti ensures the cleanliness and sanctity of this revered space where pilgrims gather for rituals and prayers. Through its efforts, the Samiti provides a peaceful, spiritually enriching experience for visitors while promoting the preservation of India’s cultural heritage.
