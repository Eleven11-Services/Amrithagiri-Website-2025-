Honoring Tradition, Building a Sustainable Tomorrow

Nestled in the majestic Himalayas, Amrithagiri Himalaya is more than just an ashram it’s a movement led by Shri Denny Amrithagiri. As one of the best NGOs in the Himalayas, it stands along the sacred banks of the Ganga, transforming lives by empowering Himalayan village communities with education and sustainable living practices. Rooted in the ancient culture of India and guided by the eternal truths of Indian philosophy, Amrithagiri is committed to preserving the cultural heritage of India while fostering progress. Through its initiatives in education NGO programs and volunteer work, it provides a platform for those passionate about Indian culture and tradition to make a meaningful impact. Join hands with one of the leading volunteer organizations and step into a journey that blends tradition with innovation nurturing both people and the planet.

About Us

Nestled in the majestic Himalayas, Amrithagiri Himalaya is more than just an ashram - it’s a movement led by Shri Denny Thomas Amrithagiri. Here, along the sacred banks of the Ganga, we are transforming lives by empowering Himalayan village communities with education and sustainable living practices. Guided by the eternal truths of Indian philosophy, Amrithagiri is on a mission to create a world where joy, unity, and love flourish for all. Step into a journey that blends tradition with innovation, and be part of a legacy that nurtures both people and planet.

Ganga Aarti

This is an ancient Hindu ritual that unites people in gratitude and devotion, emphasizing the deep connection between humanity, nature, and the divine. Rooted in the ancient culture of India and the cultural heritage of India, this tradition reflects the essence of Indian culture and tradition, bringing together communities through shared spirituality and respect for nature. Events like these are a vital part of the cultural history of India, celebrated by volunteer organizations and those passionate about volunteering for NGO initiatives that preserve these sacred practices.
